Space Atlas: Mapping The Universe and Beyond

by James Trefil and Buzz Aldrin


Book Description:

The book stands for a grand tour of our universe. Three main domains are presented including the planets, the stars, the large universe itself.
The first domain to start with our tour is the solar system. From the scorching sun to the icy vastness of Kuiper Belt beyond Pluto. Since technology is innovating, space probers or landers from Earth are capable of capturing the images of the outer-space world for Earth livers to speculate the world beyond our world.
The second domain is made up of billions of stars in our own Milky Way galaxy, where we can explore more on the galaxies beyond us and the original of stars.
The final domain we will be explore in this book is the large scale structure of the universe. We always believe that stars and planets are the clusters resulted from the interstellar collapse, however, all is being composed by the mysterious title containing the dark matter and dark energy. This domain will definitely bring readers beyond the knowledge of the ordinary, to solve the longtime unknown questions.
"Science and Nature" give readers an opportunity to ponder the operation of our universe with explicit studies conducted by experts.
