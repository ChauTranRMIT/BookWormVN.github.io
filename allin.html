All In: An Autobiography

by Billie Jean King


Book Description:

Billie Jean King elaborates her life's journey to find her true self and overcome many other hurdlers which come with her worldwide reputation as one of the greatest woman to ever play tennis. Six years as the top-ranked woman in the world, twenty Wimbledon championships, thirty-nine grand-slam titles, and the most remarkable victory named "Battle of The Exes" against Bobby Riggs. She expressively reminisces the cultural background of those years and the deep impact on her view as a woman, the assassinations and the 1960s protest against war, the civil rights movement, and ultimately the LGBTQ+ society.
Despite having troubles with finance, sexism, eating disorder and many more, she brings advices from her insights to readers on leadership, business, activism, sports, politics, marriage equality, parenting, sexuality and love. Billie Jean King concludes the story with how powerful it is to live with righteousness and an open mind. Therefore, she is bringing positive influence on her relationships and happiness.
